Importance of Reporting

Understanding why reporting child abuse is critical for protection, healing, and prevention

Why Report?

  • Access to Professional Resources Compassion has professionals, legal aid, and support services available to victims that may not otherwise have access.
  • Save a Child from Abuse Reporting can save a child from continued abuse and prevent further harm to them and others.

How Does Reporting Help?

  • 1. Recognition & Validation Reporting recognizes that something wrong happened. This is vital for a victim's recovery journey.
  • 2. Access to Resources It makes resources available to victims and their families that they otherwise would not have.
  • 3. Prevention & Safety It helps prevent future abuse and creates safety for children in the community.

"Every report matters. Your voice can be the difference between continued suffering and healing for a child."

Reporting is not just about documenting abuse—it's about taking action to protect, heal, and restore.

Protection Focus

Main Child Protection Risks

Our strategy identifies and addresses the most common risks facing children in our communities

Neglect

Children not receiving basic needs like food, care, protection, and education. The most widespread form of child endangerment.

Most common risk

Sexual Abuse

Includes exploitation, harassment, and violence against children. A severe violation requiring immediate intervention.

20.6% of cases

Physical Abuse

Beatings and physical harm by caregivers or others in the community. Leaves both visible and invisible scars.

13.5% of cases

Emotional Abuse

Shouting, humiliation, rejection, or psychological harm to children. Often overlooked but deeply damaging.

Long-term impact

Child Labour

Children forced to work instead of going to school and playing. Robs them of their childhood and future.

1.3M affected

Early Marriage & FGM

Harmful cultural practices affecting girls' development and safety. Violates their fundamental rights.

Girls at risk
