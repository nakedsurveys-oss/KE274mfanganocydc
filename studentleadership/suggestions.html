Quick Guide

Tips for great suggestions.

Help us understand and act on your ideas with these simple guidelines.

Be Specific

Instead of "improve the library," try "add more science books for Grade 6–8 students" or "extend library hours on Saturdays." Specific suggestions are easier to act on.

Focus on Solutions

While it's okay to mention problems, focus most of your suggestion on practical solutions. What would make things better? How could we implement your idea?

Think Community-Wide

Consider how your suggestion might benefit not just you, but other participants, part-time staff, or the wider school community. The broader the positive impact, the better.

Keep It Respectful

We welcome all constructive feedback. Please express your ideas respectfully, focusing on systems and processes rather than individuals.
